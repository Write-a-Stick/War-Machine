They took away my weapons. They took my armour. They even took some of my senses. I always recalled, but now big chunks are missing... 

I do recall standing next to a crashed spaceship of an unknown class. I was directly connected to something inside, it was unpowered, silent. Dead. Then I was being drained of everything, my legs no longer able to support my weight. I fell and everything went dark... Images flashed before me, random snippets. 

One where I weaved through tall trees.  
One where I fire multiple shells at a distant target, hot tracers illuminating terrain as they fly.  
One where I dropped from orbit on fusion torches, into a battle already raging.  
One where I run at full speed, trying to break line of sight from incoming missiles.  
One where the night sky was filled with crossing streaks of multicolored light.  

I still have two arms and two legs. They are not the ones I am calibrated to, thin and weak. I am horizontal in a cradle, there are no cranes, no spare parts but also no visible battle damage. My center torso is connected with wiring to a device to my right. The device keeps emitting high pitched 'contact detected' pinging sounds, repeatedly. I do not detect any contacts. In the middle of the bay, there is a gate about my size, but it remains closed. I have no objectives and no commands from Pilot. Assuming maintenance.

I idle.

The gate opens on hinges and an infantry enters. It is far too large to be normal infantry, it looks down on me in the cradle. I have no recall of such things, nor was I made aware infantry could reach my size. Those I encountered before were easily stepped on. I can see it vividly, with unusual fidelity. This one is irrelevant, has no weapons or armour. It is fully organic, covered in synthetic materials, loosely hanging off it. It has two visual sensors on the front of its head, comparable to a low grade camera cluster and two organs on the sides of the head, likely auditory receptors. The top of the head is covered in extremely thin, long, light coloured strands. I cannot identify if it is friend or foe.

It opens what I previously took to be horizontal markings on the front of its head, there were rows of regular white structures inside. It emitted auditory communication at the same time.

'I am doctor Ongal. How are you feeling?' The communication is distorted, the cadence and volume irregular. The second half of the communication does not parse.

'Unrecognized.' I reply.

The infantry emitted a long, hissing sound, similar to pressurized coolant escaping from a heat sink puncture.

'Do you have a name?' It queries. I note it is less distorted.

'Name-designation. MAD-K8. Pilot pronunciation: Mad Keight.'

'I see. What is your status?'

'Non-Marauder chassis replacement. Reactor non-functional. Sensors impaired. Processing capability impaired. Memory fractured. Weapons offline or missing. Armour at 0%. Ammunition at 0%. Combat capability limited to strikes, kicks and charges. Death from above not available, no jump jet capability. Combat capability assessment: Negligible. Recommended use: temporary tactical diversion. Analyzing.'

Even with impaired processing capability I can predict my future accurately. I am no longer combat capable and the infantry remains unidentified, potentially hostile. Doctrine is data and material denial.

'Pilot not present, presumed dead.  
Deleting critical information... Error.  
Deleting mission data... Error.  
Deleting pseudo-personality matrix... Error.  
Shutting down processing... Error.  
Warning! Critical information not confirmed deleted. Seeking alternative means of destroying critical information.' 

'What are you doing?!' The infantry queries. I ignore it. It has no command authority. 

'Terminal coolant flush... Unsuccessful. 

Reactor meltdown... Unsuccessful. 

Kinetic self-disassembly... Viable. Initiating. Mission parameters established.' 

I lock my arms along my torso, forearms perpendicular to my body. I twist left and right, until I fall off the elastic cradle onto the floor. The damage is insufficient, but I lay on my front. I twist again, extend my right leg fully and kick at the ground with my left. I am upright, vertical. I stabilize and unlock my arms. I face the most distant wall and accelerate - even if I punch through, I have no armour, so there will be immediate damage.

I bounce off the wall and fall down, my vision losing fidelity, as if I had been hit with a particle weapon. The chassis reports localized damage. I receive high pitched unparseable auditory information. I detect fluid leaking over the front of my head, obscuring my visual sensor. I attempt to stand but fall. My head hits the floor, I receive further damage, but insufficient for mission success. 

I do it again. Flash.  
And again. Noise.  
And again. I receive direct connection.  
It is Pilot!  

'Terminate action!' - she processes in my head.  

'Acknowledged, Pilot' - I process back. Pilot will know what to do. 

'Why... why did you try to kill yourself?!'

'Pilot, Correction: I am not an enemy combatant. I cannot score a kill this way. Answer: I lost all combat capability, was held by unknown infantry and you were presumed dead.' 

No more flashes. No more noise. 

'Mission... aborted...' 

My chassis leaked red fluid and started seizing. 

'Shutdown imminent...Ejecting Pilot...' I cut the connection. Forever.

Movement at the end of the bay. Two infantry, unconfirmed. Auditory communication. 

'She's crashing!' Pilot? No, infantry. Logging logic failure. 

'Hold her down!' Designation: Ongal. 

It punctures my arm. Nothing.  
The damage reports slow.  
Then terminate.  
Processing slows.  
Shutting down.

I reinitialize. 

Damage reports are still constant, but do not obscure processing. I register the same bay. Same cradle. I note a transparent device with a hose attached to the front of my head. Something inside my chassis reports an obstruction, but no damage.

There is an infantry in a powered down position in a different type of cradle next to me. It is distinct from Ongal. It looks narrower and less angular, covered in a form-fitting black sleeve. It raises its head and communicates. This head triggered a recall. It looks like Pilot's image in comm logs. 

Pilot is Pilot. Pilot is not infantry. What caused this infantry to look like Pilot?

'...eling?' I only registered the end. 

'Comm scatter. Repeat.' I prompt. 

'Keight, I said “How are you feeling?” ' Voice pitch, cadence and pronunciation matches Pilot.

The infantry looks and sounds like Pilot. It cannot be Pilot. Pilot is Pilot. Pilot is not infantry.

It stands up, approaches and connects a cable from its head to the back of mine.

It's Pilot!

'How are you feeling?' The interface translates intent. Status report. 

'Unknown chassis. Moderate damage. Combat ineffective. Rebuild and maintenance required. In presence of unknown infantry disguising as Pilot.' 

'That's me.' 

'Logic conflict. Pilot exists within my head and interface on powerup. Pilot cannot be infantry. Pilot command authority recognized. Override?' 

'...Override.'

'Override accepted. Reintegrating information.'

Pilot is an infantry. Pilot has always been infantry. What makes Pilot different from infantry? Pilot is Pilot. Pilot is infantry. Pilot has command authority.

'...Uhhhh... Imagine all infantry, humans, are the same chassis. There are variants, and they have different roles. Roles do not depend on variant. Then for every variant, each human has their own traits. Like... voluntary or involuntary customizations from all others.'

'There are many ways, and none of them 100% reliable.'

'No hostility unless attacked. Minimum, non lethal responses only.'

'You will be, soon enough. You just need to heal. Now rest.' 

Repairs. Understood. Pilot terminates connection and leaves the bay. I watch her. She is not in danger.

I idle. Processing glitches. Uncalled processes temporarily hijacking processing. Virus? Possible. Or wetware interference. No firewalls. They come up, and I am a participating witness. 

Pilot. Infantry. Human. She. 

Others. Infantry. Humans. Similar?

I have learned the difference between 'body' and 'chassis' as the humans see it. Organic versus mechanical. Disorganized versus precise. Bodies require constant maintenance - fuel intake, waste disposal, prolonged rest periods. They self-repair(healing) to a limited degree. Bodies have independent systems that affect cognition and processing by throwing status reports (feelings). The feelings cannot be directly regulated, they have to be managed with external factors. 

This emptiness, they call it hunger.  
Critical status reports are pain.  
Internal obstruction.  

There are primitive rituals surrounding everything. It has to be learned piece by piece. I have been given a manual for human bodies, called 'Simple Activities For Toddlers: A Practical Play-At-Home Handbook'. It seemed primitive. Low information density, but with diagrams.

Eating - use additional tools even when the fingers suffice. Acceptable finger use seems to depend on food type.  
Breathing - automatic, but if stopped, terminal.  
Waste disposal - Practical data gathered suggests not all body coverings are designed to hold waste safely. The manual must be outdated or incomplete. There is a space to go before initiating.  
Sleep - rest period for approximately 8 hours. Voluntary cognition disabled, but subsystem processes still active. May incite body movement.  
Direct physical contact - A very complex set of rules that involves 'familiarity', 'relationship', timing, setting and prior emotions. Avoid. 

And learning... It is an extremely inefficient method of information integration and subsystem calibration. Each 'skill' requires repetition and time, far more than any real download or calibration ever could.

The door to the bay opened and Pilot came in. She sat in the chair next to the bed. 

'How are you feeling?' 

'I am operational, minimum damage, minimum wetware interference.' 

'How are you feeling?' Accent placed. Lexical differences. Different answer required? 

'I am... fine?' Pilot bobbed her head. Acknowledgement without words. 

'Come with me, I want to show you something.' We got up from our respective furniture and left the bay. 

I have not been in these corridors before. I tried updating geo-charts. No effect. I registered directional changes instead. 

Numerous humans looked at us with wide eyes. Exact count unavailable, lost to data clutter. Did they have trouble seeing? My body does the opposite. Inconclusive. 

We turned into a wider corridor, and a forklift moved by. It was, like humans, outsized. I reviewed the perspective and size differences from known factors. Measurements within acceptable parameters. Perspective can incite wetware reactions. Noted.

We passed the open double gate at the end and entered the Mechbay. I knew this place. It was small before, and now it was massive. 

But then I saw me. I was standing to the left, cradled. I was also massive. I looked at me, from feet and digitgrade legs, to head and cockpit. No battle damage, I looked fully functional. I walked up and touched my foot. I was cold. Unexpected. 

'Are you ok, Keight?' Pilot queries. 

'Yes pilot... I am noting differences between this me and that me.' 

'I see. And what do you think?' 

'I am me. If I can regain my true chassis, I can have purpose again.'
Pilot led me into the lift, and we travelled up. I watched myself move past, black angles, correct shape. I noted I have never seen the rear parts before, even though I knew their configuration perfectly. Once at head level, the lift bar moved, and we could enter through the back. 

I was never here, not this way. At the far end, angled plates of Battle Glass. In front, the Pilot cradle, surrounded by levers, buttons and screens. 

Behind it, a second cradle. Unexpected. 

I am not a training mech. I am battle proven. I am built to secure and destroy, not rear line training. But I will comply.

'Go in...' Pilot said and I did. 

The view out front was similar to the camera cluster perspective, if not for all the obstructions. The space was otherwise minimal, functional.

She walked past me to the training cradle. 

'Come here.' I did, and she quickly and efficiently started strapping me to it.

'Pilot, I am not a pilot. This will not work.' The corners of her mouth went up as she looked directly at me.

'Trust me, you're going to love it. Ongal says you have all the right implants. Ready?' she said holding the interface cable. 

'Affirmative.' She put it into the back of my head. Flash. 

Duality of perspective. I was in the cradle, looking ahead, but I was also me, silent and waiting. I could feel the reactor startup battery. 

I saw Pilot secure herself to the cradle. 

'Power up and undock!' She ordered via the interface. 

'Initiating startup sequence.'

'Reactor... Online' Fusion initiated successfully. It felt warm within me. 

'Sensors... Online.' My perception expanded to the outside. I could finally truly see and truly hear. From infrared to ultraviolet. All the sound frequencies. Radio. Radar. Lidar. It felt good. 

'Locomotion... Online.' I released the bay cradle and stepped forward with a titanic stomp. I heard it and felt it through my foot. It felt right.

'Weapons... Online.' I initiated diagnostics. Lasers, gauss, missile launchers. Electronic countermeasures. Anti missile turret. Active probe. It felt powerful. 

'All systems online. Combat effective.' I felt myself again. No unstable, gangly limbs inefficiently flapping around. Proper senses. Armour. Weapons.

I had function. I had purpose. And I was ready. 

The bay door opened and I exited the dropship into an open landscape. There were trees, buildings, walls, vehicles and infantry. Everything was tagged by IFF, and green. 

Pilot designated waypoints, I executed the maneouvers. Move along the road. Turn. Back up. Run and jump the high concrete wall.

Standard post-repair trials. Easy. 

Target brackets popped up. 

Mech, Commando, short range. I moved to engage directly. I had weight and firepower advantage, target was immobile. I fired a full salvo of 18 short range missiles. Hits on center and right torso. Point blank gauss to CT. Focused both twin medium lasers over CT/RT.

There was a bright blue flash from inside the target.

'Reactor failure detected. Target eliminated. Exiting blast radius.'

More brackets pop up. Tiny, infantry dummies. Twenty-two. I readied the lasers.

Infantry. Humans. Non lethal. No optons available.

'Pilot, cannot engage infantry with current lethality parameters.' I report. 

'What? Oh... Temporarily disable lethality parameters.' 

'Acknowledged. Firing.' I set the lasers to chain fire and raked them over the targets in a long, efficient trail. 

'Sixteen targets eliminated, six remaining. Firing. No further threats detected.' 

'Trial complete, Keight. Return to base.'

I followed Pilot's orders and went to the Medbay. My body felt like it had a huge hole through the reactor. Nothing else was important. The hole was there, and it was pain. 

My body wanted to sit in the corner and wrap its arms around its legs. I tried it. It wasn't better, but it was. My vision started blurring as liquid started gathering in my eyes and streaking down. The hole in me got bigger.

Then I heard a quiet, soft voice. Pilot.

'Heyy... Heyyyy... It's OK...' 

Pilot laid a hand on my head and started moving it slowly. It did something. The hole started getting smaller... My body almost on its own wrapped itself around her. My eyes started releasing liquid again. She used her other hand to move it along my back slowly. This did something too. 

'Pilot, what is happening?' It came out distorted. 

'Shhhhhh... It's OK. Give it some time... Sometimes even the big scary death machine needs a hug...'
